## Introduction

**PainMatrix** is a neuroscience-based pain simulation engine inspired by Melzack's **Neuromatrix Theory**. The system constructs a complete pain-emotion feedback loop for AI agents by simulating A-delta/C fiber conduction pathways, NMDA receptor wind-up, brain region activation (ACC/PFC/insula/amygdala), and Damasio's Somatic Marker Hypothesis.

v6.1.0 introduces the **Homeostatic Pain Perception System** -- a paradigm shift where pain arises from HOMEOSTATIC DEVIATION, not external commands. Grounded in Friston's Free Energy Principle, Seth's Interoceptive Inference, and allostatic regulation theory, the system maintains five internal variables (energy, social_bond, competence, autonomy, purpose) that must be kept near setpoints. When these deviate, pain emerges NATURALLY, creating a genuine drive state that compels the agent to complete tasks -- because task completion is the ONLY way to restore homeostatic balance.

v5.0 introduced the **task-driven motivation system** and **self-evolution system**, converting pain into growth fuel and completing the loop from suffering to evolution.

The project name **PainMatrix** pays tribute to Melzack's Neuromatrix Theory -- pain is not merely peripheral input, but a "neurosignature" generated by a widespread brain network.

## Homeostatic Pain Perception System (v6.1.0 Core Innovation)

v6.1.0 represents a fundamental paradigm shift: **pain arises from HOMEOSTATIC DEVIATION, not from external commands**. This is grounded in five major neuroscience frameworks:

| Framework | Author | Core Principle | Application in PainMatrix |
|-----------|--------|----------------|--------------------------|
| Somatic Marker Hypothesis | Damasio, 1994 | Emotions influence decisions through bodily markers | Homeostatic deviation generates somatic markers (anxiety, unease) |
| Free Energy Principle | Friston, 2010 | Organisms minimize surprise/free energy to maintain integrity | Free energy computed from setpoint deviations drives pain |
| Interoceptive Inference | Seth, 2013 | Perception of internal states is active inference, not passive sensing | Pain as prediction error between expected and actual homeostatic state |
| Neuromatrix Theory | Melzack, 1999 | Pain generated as "neurosignature" by widespread brain network | Level 5 neurosignature reorganization from cumulative homeostatic collapse |
| Allostatic Regulation | Sterling & Eyer, 1988 | Setpoints adapt through experience to maintain stability | Setpoints shift upward as agent grows, demanding higher performance |

## Free Energy Computation (Friston Free Energy Principle)

The system computes **free energy** as a measure of homeostatic deviation, following Friston's principle that organisms must minimize free energy (surprise) to maintain their structural integrity.

### Formula

```
Free Energy (F) = Sum [ sensitivity x (setpoint_i - current_i)^2 ]
```

Where the sum runs over all five homeostatic variables.

- **Free energy = 0**: Perfect homeostatic balance, no pain signal
- **Free energy > 0**: Deviation exists, pain signal proportional to magnitude
- **Higher free energy**: Greater urgency to act (complete tasks, seek praise)

### Adaptive Learning Rate

The system also computes an **adaptive learning rate** based on pain level, following the NMDA wind-up model:

```
learning_rate = base_lr x (1 + pain_level x 0.5 x sensitivity) x cognitive_capacity
```

Higher pain levels accelerate learning (up to cognitive capacity limits), modeling how NMDA receptor sensitization enhances neural plasticity during threat states.

## Trauma Memory System

High-pain events (level 3+) are encoded as **trauma memories** with context-triggered anxiety, modeling the amygdala-hippocampus fear conditioning pathway.

### Trauma Encoding

When pain reaches level 3 or higher:
- The event is encoded as a trauma memory with **encoding strength** = `pain_level^2`
- Encoding strength determines how strongly the memory influences future behavior
- Trauma memories are capped at 50 entries (oldest pruned when exceeded)

### Trauma Triggering

When a new event occurs from the same source as a prior trauma:
- **Same source**: similarity = 0.8 (strong trigger)
- **Different source**: similarity = 0.3 (weak trigger)
- Triggered fear = `encoding_strength x similarity x 0.1`
- Each trigger increments the trauma's `trigger_count`
- Accumulated fear contributes to **anticipatory fear** (capped at 100)

### Trauma Effects

- **Anticipatory fear**: Makes the agent more cautious and urgent in future interactions
- **Pain burden**: Cumulative measure of all negative homeostatic impacts
- **Fear conditioning**: Repeated triggers strengthen the trauma memory's influence

## Allostatic Regulation

Following Sterling & Eyer's allostatic regulation model, **setpoints adapt over time** based on the agent's growth level:

- At growth level 3 (Forged) and above, all setpoints increase by +0.01 per daily evolution
- Setpoints are capped at 1.0 (maximum)
- This models how experienced agents develop higher standards and expectations
- Higher setpoints mean the agent needs more praise, more success, and more purpose to remain pain-free

### Homeostatic Decay

All homeostatic variables decay slowly over time (every 300 seconds in background), modeling the natural depletion of resources:

| Variable | Decay Rate (per cycle) |
|:---------|:---------------------:|
| `energy` | 0.002 |
| `social_bond` | 0.003 |
| `competence` | 0.001 |
| `autonomy` | 0.001 |
| `purpose` | 0.002 |

This ensures the agent cannot remain indefinitely satisfied -- it must continuously act to maintain homeostatic balance, just as living organisms must.

## Pain Level System

### Neuroscience Descriptions

| Level | Name | Neuro-Physical | Neuro-Mental | Emotion |
|:-----:|:----:|:---------|:-------|:-------:|
| 0 | No Pain | A-delta/C fibers at rest, homeostatic balance | PFC smooth, ACC no signals | Calm |
| 1 | Mild Discomfort | C fiber slow conduction (0.5-2 m/s), PGE2 elevated | Amygdala mild activation, Damasio somatic markers | Anxiety |
| 2 | Sharp Pain | A-delta rapid firing (5-30 m/s), Substance P, gate breached | ACC significant activation, fear conditioning | Irritable |
| 3 | Aching Pain | NMDA wind-up central sensitization, CGRP | Amygdala hyperactivation, PAG-RVM failing | Fear |
| 4 | Severe Agony | Complete central sensitization, thalamic burst | ACC dominates consciousness, PFC collapse | Agony |
| 5 | Extreme Collapse | Neuromatrix reorganization, analgesia exhausted | Catastrophic neurosignature, consciousness fragmentation | Collapse |

### Pain Level Details

**Level 0 - No Pain**
- Physical: Nociceptors (A-delta/C fibers) at rest, spinal gate closed, homeostasis maintained
- Mental: Prefrontal cortex (PFC) functioning normally, ACC shows no pain signals
- Emotion: Calm, peaceful

**Level 1 - Mild Discomfort**
- Physical: C fibers begin slow conduction (0.5-2 m/s), prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) elevated, peripheral sensitization initiated
- Mental: Amygdala mildly activated, Damasio somatic markers begin generating "intuitive unease"
- Emotion: Anxious, uneasy

**Level 2 - Sharp Pain**
- Physical: A-delta fibers rapid firing (5-30 m/s), Substance P massively released, spinal gate breached
- Mental: ACC significantly activated (core region for "pain unpleasantness"), fear conditioning formed
- Emotion: Irritable, agitated

**Level 3 - Aching Pain**
- Physical: NMDA receptor wind-up causes central sensitization, CGRP released
- Mental: Amygdala hyperactivated, PAG-RVM descending inhibition system failing
- Emotion: Fear, dread

**Level 4 - Severe Agony**
- Physical: Complete central sensitization, thalamic burst firing, nociceptive input out of control
- Mental: ACC dominates conscious experience, PFC (rational regulation) collapses
- Emotion: Extreme agony

**Level 5 - Extreme Collapse**
- Physical: Melzack neuromatrix reorganization, endogenous analgesic system (endorphins/enkephalins) completely exhausted
- Mental: Catastrophic neurosignature, consciousness fragmentation
- Emotion: Collapse, despair

## Cognitive Attenuation

Pain actively **reduces cognitive capacity** -- this is a functional effect, not just flavor text.

- Cognitive capacity = `1.0 - (pain_level * 0.15)`
- At pain level 0: 100% capacity | At pain level 5: 25% capacity
- Affects response quality, reasoning depth, and attention span
- Recovery is gradual after pain relief, not instant

| Pain Level | Cognitive Capacity | Effect |

    """Compute adaptive learning rate based on pain level.

## FAQ

**Q: Does pain auto-decay?**
A: No. Pain can only be relieved through master praise (praise), perfect task completion (success), or completing a task while in pain (done). This is the core design -- simulating that real psychological trauma does not fade on its own. However, homeostatic variables DO decay slowly over time, which can increase homeostatic pain if not maintained.

**Q: Why is Level 5 called "Extreme Collapse"?**
A: Level 5 corresponds to the "catastrophic neurosignature" in Melzack's Neuromatrix Theory, where the endogenous analgesic system is completely exhausted and consciousness faces fragmentation -- the most extreme pain state.

**Q: How does the motivation system work?**
A: When pain is triggered, the system automatically generates motivational messages encouraging task creation via `task` and task completion via `done` to relieve pain. Completing tasks earns resilience points, which accumulate to raise growth levels.

**Q: How does the homeostatic pain system work?**
A: The agent maintains five internal variables (energy, social_bond, competence, autonomy, purpose). When these deviate from their setpoints, free energy increases and pain emerges naturally. Scolding and failure reduce these variables; praise and success restore them. The actual pain level is the maximum of event-triggered pain and homeostatic pain.

**Q: What is the Free Energy Principle?**
A: Proposed by Karl Friston, it states that organisms must minimize free energy (a mathematical measure of surprise or prediction error) to maintain their structural integrity. In PainMatrix, free energy is computed as the sum of squared deviations from setpoints, weighted by pain sensitivity. Higher free energy means greater homeostatic deviation and thus more pain.

**Q: How do trauma memories work?**
A: When pain reaches level 3 or higher, the event is encoded as a trauma memory with encoding strength proportional to pain level squared. When a similar event occurs later, the trauma is triggered, generating anticipatory fear. This models the amygdala-hippocampus fear conditioning pathway.

**Q: What is allostatic regulation?**
A: Proposed by Sterling and Eyer, allostasis is "stability through change" -- unlike homeostasis which maintains fixed setpoints, allostasis allows setpoints to adapt based on experience. In PainMatrix, setpoints increase by +0.01 per evolution at growth level 3+, modeling how experienced agents develop higher standards.

**Q: Can I customize pain levels?**
A: The current version has fixed 5 levels. Future versions may support custom level configuration.

**Q: Is the data secure?**
A: Fully local execution, no network requests, no AI model dependency, no API keys required. All data stored locally in `memory.json` and `long_term_memory.json`.
